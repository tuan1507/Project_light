Our History

Most loved steak house since 1985

FarmHouse is a restaurant chain that has its origins in the United States. The first FarmHouse restaurant was opened in 2005 in Greenville, South Carolina. The founders, Michael and Paula Zink, wanted to create a dining experience that celebrated the values of farm-to-table cuisine, using fresh and locally sourced ingredients.

The restaurant's menu features a range of dishes that are inspired by traditional American cuisine, such as fried chicken, meatloaf, and mac and cheese. Over the years, FarmHouse has expanded to several locations across the country, including Florida, North Carolina, and Texas.

The restaurant chain has received numerous accolades for its commitment to sustainability, including being named a Certified Green Restaurant® by the Green Restaurant Association. Today, FarmHouse continues to be a popular destination for diners who are looking for delicious food made with locally sourced ingredients and served in a warm and inviting atmosphere
